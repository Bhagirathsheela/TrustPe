## 1. What TrustPe is

TrustPe is a closed-membership software platform that helps small groups
of people who already know each other arrange personal loans between
themselves. The platform records the terms the parties agree to, lets
them sign a click-wrap agreement, and tracks repayment status.

**TrustPe is NOT:**

- A bank, NBFC, NBFC-P2P, banking correspondent, or any kind of
  RBI-regulated entity.
- An investment platform, a securities exchange, or anything that should
  ever be described as "investing".
- A custodian of your money. Money moves directly from one member to
  another over UPI. TrustPe never holds, pools, escrows, or routes
  member funds.
- A guarantor of repayment. TrustPe does not promise that a loan will be
  repaid. TrustPe does not insure loans. TrustPe will not pay you on a
  defaulted loan.
- An advice service. TrustPe does not tell you who to lend to, who not
  to lend to, what interest rate to charge, or how much to lend.

By using TrustPe you acknowledge and accept the above without
qualification.

## 4. Loans on TrustPe — your relationship

A loan arranged through TrustPe is a contract between **you and the other
member**. TrustPe is not a party to the loan. TrustPe records the terms
the two of you agree to and stores a signed click-wrap agreement, which
the two of you can rely on as evidence of what was agreed.

When you publish a loan request, fund an offer, or sign an agreement, you
are entering a legally binding contract with the counter-party. We
strongly recommend that you only deal with people you know personally
and trust.

### 4.1 Interest rate cap

The interest rate you agree to is capped by the moneylending law of the
state listed on the borrower's profile. The app shows the applicable cap
when an offer is being funded and the cap is restated in the agreement.
Any agreement attempting to exceed the cap is unenforceable to the
extent of the excess (Indian Contract Act §23).

### 4.2 Maximum amount and tenure (Phase 1)

Phase 1 pilot limits each loan to:

- Minimum: ₹5,000
- Maximum: ₹50,000
- Tenure: 1 to 6 months

These limits will change in future phases.

### 4.3 No promises of return

Lenders, you accept that:

- The borrower may default. If they do, you bear the loss.
- TrustPe does not promise a return, a yield, or anything like it.
- TrustPe will not compensate you for a defaulted loan.

If you ever see anyone — including a TrustPe representative — describing
TrustPe as an "investment opportunity", "guaranteed returns",
"liquidity", or any similar language, please report it to

## 5. How the money moves

All loan payments — initial disbursal and every EMI — move directly from
the payer's UPI app to the receiver's UPI VPA. TrustPe does not see, hold,
collect, or have any control over the money.

The app records:

- That the payer claims to have sent the payment, with a UTR you must
  enter
- That the receiver has either confirmed receipt or has disputed it

If you mark a payment as sent without actually sending it, you commit
fraud against the receiver and may also commit a criminal offence. If
you receive a payment but mark it as not received, you may be liable to
the sender for the disputed amount.

The platform operator is not responsible for verifying UTRs against your
bank statement. The receiver does that.

## 6. Disputes between members

If the receiver disputes a payment after the payer says it was sent, the
loan enters a "disputed" state. The platform operator will attempt to
resolve the dispute by reviewing both parties' evidence (screenshots,
UTRs, bank-statement excerpts the parties choose to share).

The operator's resolution decisions in the app are about the **app's
internal state** — marking a payment as verified, failed, or marking a
loan as defaulted. The operator's decision does not, and cannot, change
the legal rights and obligations between you and the counter-party.
Either of you may still pursue civil remedies in court, just as you
could outside the app.

## 7. TrustScore, reviews, and reputation

After a loan completes (closed or defaulted), each party can rate and
review the other. Reviews are visible on the reviewee's public profile
inside TrustPe.

You agree that:

- You will write reviews truthfully and based on actual experience.
- You will not write reviews that are defamatory or that disclose the

- You will not write reviews intended to extort or to coerce.

TrustScore is an algorithmically computed reputation score on a 300–900
scale. It is updated as you participate, receive reviews, and either
honour or default on loans. TrustScore is for in-app display only — it
is not a credit score, it is not reported to any credit bureau, and it
should not be treated as a substitute for one.

We may correct or remove a review if it violates these Terms, after a
fair hearing.

## 18. Open items for legal review

1. **State moneylending acts and the platform's exposure.** The draft
   treats the per-state ROI cap as binding on the borrower-lender
   contract. Counsel to confirm the operator faces no separate liability
   under those acts, and that documenting the cap in the agreement is
   sufficient.
2. **PMLA Reporting Entity classification.** The draft assumes TrustPe is
   not a Reporting Entity in Phase 1. Counsel to confirm and to add a
   trigger condition for re-evaluation if the operator's role expands.
3. **NBFC-P2P direction applicability.** Counsel to confirm whether RBI's
   NBFC-P2P direction reaches a platform that does not intermediate
   funds.
4. **Closed user group / friends-and-family designation.** Counsel to
   advise on whether the closed-membership designation must be more
   prominent at signup, on the home screen, and inside the agreement.
5. **Consumer protection.** Counsel to advise whether members count as
   "consumers" under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 and what that
   implies for dispute resolution and venue clauses.
6. **Stamp duty on agreements.** Phase 1 ships a click-wrap loan
   agreement with a SHA-256 hash but no physical stamp paper. Counsel
   to advise (a) whether the agreement is admissible in evidence in the
   relevant state without stamping, (b) whether a Sec 35 Indian Stamp
   Act exception applies, and (c) what wording in section 16 of these
   Terms or in the agreement itself should reflect this.
7. **Arbitration clause.** The draft picks ordinary courts; counsel to
   advise on whether an arbitration clause (and seat) would be more
   appropriate for member-vs-operator disputes.
8. **Limitation of liability cap.** Confirm the ₹500 floor + 12-month
   fees ceiling is enforceable; some High Court decisions have read down
   tiny caps.
9. **Indian Contract Act §23 reference.** Confirm the section number is
   correct and the principle (agreement defeating statute is void)
   applies as drafted.
10. **Section 8 (prohibited acts) and IT Act §66/§66C/§66D linkage.**
    Where a Terms breach is also a criminal offence, counsel to confirm
    we have the wording and the right to report.
